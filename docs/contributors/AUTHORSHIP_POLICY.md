# Authorship Policy

## 1. Purpose

This document defines Permea Core's initial policy for distinguishing contributor credit, acknowledgements, reviewer credit, and paper authorship.

It is a project operating policy, not legal advice, employment advice, institutional policy, or a journal-specific authorship guarantee.

## 2. Contributor Credit vs Authorship Distinction

Contributor credit records useful participation in the project.

Paper authorship records substantial contribution to a specific manuscript or scientific work, plus accountability and final approval.

These are related but not automatic equivalents.

## 3. Authorship Candidate Criteria

An authorship candidate should generally have made a substantial contribution to a specific paper or evidence package and be able to take responsibility for assigned parts of the work.

Authorship candidates should have a documented contribution record before final submission.

## 4. Paper Authorship Principles

Paper authorship may be appropriate when a contributor provides:

- substantial scientific or methodological contribution
- analysis or interpretation contribution
- manuscript drafting or substantive revision contribution
- accountability for assigned sections or artifacts
- final approval before submission

Authorship decisions must be made manually for each paper.

## 5. CRediT-Style Contribution Categories

Permea may use CRediT-style roles conceptually to describe contributions. This does not mean a journal has formally adopted or approved the role list for a given paper.

Relevant categories may include:

- conceptualization
- methodology
- software
- validation
- formal analysis
- investigation
- data curation
- writing original draft
- writing review/editing
- visualization
- supervision/project administration

The category label should describe what happened; it should not inflate contribution scope.

## 6. Contributions That May Support Authorship

Examples that may support authorship, depending on scope and accountability:

- designing a benchmark central to the manuscript
- implementing or validating core analysis used in the paper
- performing substantial formal analysis
- curating a dataset with documented provenance and release review
- producing key figures or result artifacts
- drafting major manuscript sections
- materially revising scientific framing or interpretation
- providing documented wet-lab data central to the paper
- leading source-to-claim review for core claims

These examples support authorship consideration; they do not guarantee authorship.

## 7. Contributions That Usually Support Acknowledgement Only

Examples that usually support acknowledgement or contributor credit rather than authorship:

- typo fixes
- small documentation edits
- minor bug reports
- general encouragement
- informal advice
- brief reviewer comments
- issue triage without substantive scientific contribution
- using the repository
- suggesting a paper or tool without deeper contribution

Substantiality and accountability determine whether a contribution may move beyond acknowledgement.

## 8. Reviewer Credit Policy

Reviewer contributions may be credited when they materially improve:

- claim boundaries
- method clarity
- benchmark interpretation
- reproducibility
- dataset/provenance caution
- manuscript limitations
- paper/source-to-claim alignment

Reviewer credit may appear as acknowledgement, contributor record, or authorship consideration depending on depth, responsibility, and final manual decision.

## 9. Wet-Lab Collaboration Authorship Considerations

Wet-lab collaborators may be authorship candidates when they contribute material experimental design, data generation, data interpretation, assay validation, or manuscript work.

Wet-lab collaboration does not automatically grant authorship. Authorship should reflect:

- role in assay design
- role in data generation
- role in data interpretation
- role in writing/revision
- ability to approve and be accountable for assigned content
- institutional or partner constraints

## 10. Dispute Handling Placeholder

Authorship disputes should be handled through a documented manual process.

Until a formal governance process exists, disputes should be escalated to project maintainers and founder/manual approvers without making public accusations or exposing private correspondence.

This document does not make legal conclusions about employment, institutional IP, or authorship disputes.

## 11. Final Manual Approval Requirement

Final paper authorship requires manual approval before submission.

Before submission, the project should record:

- authors
- affiliations
- contribution roles
- acknowledgements
- conflicts or unresolved issues
- final approval status

## 12. No Automatic Authorship from GitHub PRs

Merged pull requests, issue comments, code commits, reviews, and discussions do not automatically create paper authorship.

They may support contributor credit, acknowledgement, or authorship consideration when they meet the paper-specific criteria above.

## Claim-Boundary Reminder

Permea may use "AlphaFold for Delivery" as ambition or positioning only.

Permea must not claim AlphaFold-level performance, adoption, or standardization; completed wet-lab validation unless documented; clinical efficacy; universal delivery prediction; production-grade drug delivery platform status; or dataset redistribution permission without source/license approval.

Benchmark claims must remain scoped to dataset, split, metric, model, artifact, and evidence level.

Dry-lab contributions do not equal biological validation. Wet-lab collaboration interest does not equal wet-lab validation.
