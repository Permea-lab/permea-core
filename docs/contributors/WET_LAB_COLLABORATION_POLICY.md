# Wet-Lab Collaboration Policy

## 1. Purpose

This document defines Permea Core's initial wet-lab collaboration policy.

The goal is to support future experimental collaboration while preventing premature validation claims, unclear data rights, and ambiguous authorship or publication expectations.

## 2. Wet-Lab Collaboration Status

Permea is dry-lab-first today.

Wet-lab collaboration may be pursued, but interest, discussion, protocol review, or planned experiments do not equal completed wet-lab validation.

## 3. Collaboration Levels

### W0 Informal Advisor

Provides informal context, suggestions, or scientific feedback without responsibility for assay work or data generation.

### W1 Protocol Reviewer

Reviews proposed assays, protocols, controls, or feasibility but does not generate data.

### W2 Independent Assay Partner

Runs an independent assay or experiment under separately agreed terms.

### W3 Dataset Contributor

Contributes experimental data, assay metadata, or wet-lab-derived datasets.

### W4 Co-Development Lab

Co-develops experimental design, data generation, interpretation, and manuscript or release materials.

### W5 Institutional Partner

Participates through a university, nonprofit, lab, or institution with formal collaboration, data, publication, or IP terms.

## 4. University / Nonprofit Collaboration Principles

University and nonprofit collaborations should define:

- scientific objective
- collaborator roles
- data ownership
- publication expectations
- attribution and authorship expectations
- release permission
- confidentiality or embargo terms
- institutional review needs, if any
- material or data transfer requirements, if any

Do not assume that informal academic or nonprofit participation grants public release permission.

## 5. Assay / Protocol Documentation Requirements

Wet-lab collaboration records should document:

- assay purpose
- biological context
- protocol version
- controls
- sample or candidate identity rules
- replicate strategy
- readout definition
- success/failure criteria
- limitations
- responsible lab or partner

Assay claims should remain tied to the documented assay and context.

## 6. Data / Provenance Requirements

Wet-lab data contributions should document:

- source lab or partner
- data owner
- generation date or version
- assay protocol reference
- raw vs processed data status
- release permission
- attribution requirements
- confidentiality restrictions
- label or readout definitions
- processing steps
- quality-control notes
- evidence level

Wet-lab datasets must follow `docs/scientific-governance/DATASET_POLICY.md`.

## 7. IP / Publication / Permission Placeholder

Wet-lab collaborations may involve institutional IP, material transfer, publication review, confidentiality, or data-use constraints.

These must be resolved manually before public claims, public data release, or public manuscript submission.

This policy does not make legal conclusions about employment, institutional IP, material transfer, publication rights, or authorship disputes.

## 8. Evidence Ladder Implications

Wet-lab data may change evidence level only when documented experimental evidence exists.

Use `docs/EVIDENCE-LADDER.md` to distinguish:

- computational evidence
- reproducible benchmark evidence
- literature-grounded plausibility
- in vitro evidence
- stronger biological validation

Do not upgrade claim level based on collaboration status alone.

## 9. Claim Upgrade Rules

Wet-lab validation claims are allowed only after documented experimental evidence exists.

No in vivo, therapeutic, or clinical claims may be made unless the corresponding evidence is documented and approved.

A wet-lab result should state:

- assay context
- candidate or sample scope
- readout
- controls
- limitations
- evidence level
- release approval status

## 10. Authorship / Acknowledgement Considerations

Wet-lab collaborators may be acknowledged or considered for authorship depending on:

- assay design contribution
- data generation contribution
- data interpretation contribution
- manuscript contribution
- responsibility for assigned content
- final approval
- partner or institutional requirements

Authorship is not automatic and must follow `docs/contributors/AUTHORSHIP_POLICY.md`.

## 11. Material / Data Transfer Caution

Do not transfer or publish materials, datasets, protocols, unpublished results, or partner records without documented permission.

If material transfer, data-use, confidentiality, or institutional terms may apply, pause public release until the relevant manual review is complete.

## 12. Release Review Requirements

Wet-lab-related release must pass:

- claim review
- dataset/provenance review
- public-safe artifact review
- paper alignment review
- release review
- manual/founder/maintainer approval where required
- partner or institutional approval where applicable

Use `docs/release/RELEASE_REVIEW_PROCESS.md` and `docs/release/PUBLIC_RELEASE_CHECKLIST.md`.

## 13. What Must Be Resolved Before Public Wet-Lab Claims

Before public wet-lab claims, resolve:

- assay documentation
- data provenance
- data ownership
- release permission
- attribution and authorship expectations
- confidentiality or embargo terms
- evidence level
- source-to-claim support
- manuscript/README/release alignment
- manual approval

If these are unresolved, keep claims internal, provisional, or omitted.

## Claim-Boundary Reminder

Permea may use "AlphaFold for Delivery" as ambition or positioning only.

Permea must not claim AlphaFold-level performance, adoption, or standardization; completed wet-lab validation unless documented; clinical efficacy; universal delivery prediction; production-grade drug delivery platform status; or dataset redistribution permission without source/license approval.

Benchmark claims must remain scoped to dataset, split, metric, model, artifact, and evidence level.

Dry-lab contributions do not equal biological validation. Wet-lab collaboration interest does not equal wet-lab validation.
