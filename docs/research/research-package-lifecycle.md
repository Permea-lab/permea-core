# Research Package Lifecycle

Research package lifecycle status is about review maturity for package assembly. It does not imply paper acceptance, biological efficacy, experimental validation, therapeutic outcome, clinical evidence, expression improvement, or solved delivery.

## Statuses

| Status | Entry criteria | Required evidence | Required datasets | Required benchmark links | Required reproducibility checks | Allowed public claims | Prohibited claims | Exit criteria |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Proposed | Package idea, package type, intended audience, and purpose are described. | Evidence needs are listed. | Dataset needs are listed if known. | Benchmark needs are listed if known. | Reproducibility path is proposed or marked not yet demonstrated. | May state that a research package surface is proposed. | No paper, report-result, validation, submission, publication, experimental validation, clinical evidence, or solved-delivery claim. | Draft package metadata is created. |
| Draft Package | Template fields are partially populated. | Linked evidence entries are identified. | Linked datasets are identified or gaps recorded. | Linked benchmarks are identified or gaps recorded. | Validation commands are identified. | May state that package metadata is being drafted. | No internal reproducibility, public review, external review, submitted, or published claim. | Required links and reproduction commands are complete enough for internal reproduction. |
| Internally Reproducible | Maintainer can reproduce the package artifacts locally. | Evidence records and generated artifacts are linked. | Dataset cards and provenance records are linked. | Benchmark cards and registry entries are linked. | Local reproduction and validation commands pass. | May state that package artifacts reproduce locally. | No public review readiness, external review, submission, publication, experimental validation, or clinical evidence claim. | Claim-boundary review and public package review are completed. |
| Public Review Package | Package is ready for public technical review. | Evidence map, reports, limitations, and claim boundaries are linked. | Dataset status and provenance limits are linked. | Benchmark status and evidence limits are linked. | Full public validation and reproduction commands pass. | May state that the package is ready for public technical review. | No external review ready, submitted, published, biological efficacy, therapeutic outcome, or solved-delivery claim. | External review checklist is completed. |
| External Review Ready | Package has complete public review materials and external-review checklist. | Evidence and limitation records are complete for the stated package scope. | Dataset provenance and usage constraints are complete for the stated scope. | Benchmark lifecycle status and limitations are complete for the stated scope. | Reproduction and validation outputs are current. | May state that the package is ready for external technical review. | No submitted, published, experimental validation, clinical evidence, or biological outcome claim. | Submission decision or external review outcome is recorded. |
| Submitted | Package or paper has been submitted through a separately documented process. | Submitted package evidence bundle is fixed. | Dataset references are fixed for the submission. | Benchmark references are fixed for the submission. | Submitted package validation record is fixed. | May state submitted status only with review record. | No acceptance, publication, clinical evidence, experimental validation, or biological outcome claim unless separately supported. | Publication, rejection, revision, supersession, or archive status is recorded. |
| Published | Package or paper has a public publication record. | Publication-linked evidence bundle is fixed. | Publication-linked dataset references are fixed. | Publication-linked benchmark references are fixed. | Publication-linked reproducibility record is fixed. | May state the publication record and bounded package scope. | No broader biological, clinical, therapeutic, or performance claim beyond the published scope and evidence. | Superseded or archived when replaced. |
| Superseded | Newer package replaces this package. | Supersession reason and successor package are linked. | Affected dataset links are recorded. | Affected benchmark links are recorded. | Final reproduction status is retained. | May state that a package is superseded. | No current active package claim. | Archived or replaced. |
| Archived | Package is retained for historical traceability. | Archive record and final evidence links are retained. | Final dataset links are retained. | Final benchmark links are retained. | Final validation state is retained. | May state that a package is archived. | No current-use, active-review, submitted-current, or published-current claim. | Reopening requires new lifecycle review. |

## Review Rule

Promoting a research package status requires updating the registry, package metadata, linked evidence, linked benchmarks, linked datasets, linked specifications, linked claims, reproducibility path, validation path, limitations, and claim boundaries in the same review.
